Scoring

  • A match consists of the best of three games.
  • Each game is played to 21 points, with the side that first scores 21 points winning that game.
  • f the score reaches 20-20, the game continues until one side gains a two-point lead.

Serving

  • The right to serve is determined by a coin toss or rally.
  • The server must stand within the service court and hit the shuttlecock below the waist with the racquet.
  • The serve must be diagonal, from one service court to the other.
  • The server continues to serve until they make a fault (e.g., the shuttlecock lands outside the boundaries).

Doubles Play

  • A point is scored when the shuttlecock lands within the opponent's court boundaries.
  • If a fault is committed (e.g., the shuttlecock lands out of bounds, a player touches the net, or the shuttlecock is hit twice), the opponent scores a point.

Change of Ends

  • Players or teams change ends at the end of each game and when one side reaches 11 points in the third game.

Let

  • If a rally is hindered by circumstances beyond the players' control (e.g., a shuttlecock getting caught in the net), the rally is declared a "let," and the point is replayed.

Faults

A fault is declared if a player:

  • Fails to serve or receive within the boundaries of the court.
  • Fails to hit the shuttlecock over the net and into the opponent's court.
  • Touches the net with the racquet or body.
  • Hits the shuttlecock before it crosses the net.
  • Hits the shuttlecock twice successively.
